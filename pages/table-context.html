URC table context

The one-point gap is the strongest match angle.

During the research pass on April 14, 2026, the URC public table snapshot showed Ulster Rugby on 47 points and Leinster Rugby on 46. That made the derby a third-versus-fourth style story in the visible table picture.

3Ulster Rugby47 pts
4Leinster Rugby46 pts

This should be handled as a snapshot, not a permanent table claim. Standings can change quickly, especially around a weekend round. For the article angle, the point is still useful: the fixture was not just a rivalry match. It also had clear table pressure attached to it when researched.

That context changes how I would watch the game. A derby can already become emotional, but a one-point table gap makes every swing in territory, penalty count, and set-piece pressure feel heavier.
